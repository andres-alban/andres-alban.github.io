Postdoctoral Research Fellow

AALBAN at mgh.harvard edu

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Institute for Technology (ITA). My research uses stochastic simulation and optimization to support decision-making in healthcare settings. I have worked on clinical trial design, ICU patient-flow, and resource allocation of mobile healthcare units. At the ITA, he is working with Dr. Carrie Lubitz on the optimization of thyroid nodule treatment.

I received my Ph.D. in Operations Management from INSEAD in 2021 supervised by Prof. Stephen E. Chick. During my Ph.D. studies at INSEAD, I participated in the European Sepsis Academy (ESA), a European Union funded project. Through this project I collaborated in medical research at the Amsterdam University Medical Center and University of Athens. I received my undergraduate degree in applied mathematics and physics from the New Jersey Institute of Technology and participated.
