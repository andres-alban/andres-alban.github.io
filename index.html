I am Ph.D. candidate in the Technology and Operations Management area at INSEAD. My research work has applications in health care using methodology from (Bayesian) statistics, stochastic simulation, optimization, and data analytics. I have a particular interest in intensive care and optimal learning in clinical trials.

I received my undergraduate degree in applied mathematics and physics from the New Jersey Institute of Technology. During my Ph.D. studies at INSEAD, I participated in the European Sepsis Academy (ESA), a European Union funded project. Through this project I collaborated in medical research at the Amsterdam University Medical Center and University of Athens and attended medical workshops and conferences.

I am currently in the academic job market. I presented the following talks at the INFORMS Annual Meeting:

  • A value of information approach to designing sequential clinical trials for precision medicine (Session WB30, Nov. 11 12:30PM EST)
  • Value-based clinical trials: selecting trial lengths and recruitment rates in different regulatory contexts (Session FB41, Nov. 13 12:30PM EST)
