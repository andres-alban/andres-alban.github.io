Andres Alban

Postdoctoral Research Fellow

MGH Institute for Technology Assessment, Harvard Medical School

aalban@mgh.harvard.edu

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the MGH Institute for Technology (ITA), Harvard Medical School. My research uses stochastic simulation and optimization to support decision-making in healthcare settings. I have worked on clinical trial design, ICU patient-flow, resource allocation of mobile healthcare units. At the ITA, I am working on treatment and screening of thyroid and lung cancer, supervised by Carrie Lubitz and Jagpreet Chhatwal.

I received my Ph.D. in Operations Management from INSEAD in 2021 supervised by Prof. Stephen E. Chick. During my Ph.D. studies at INSEAD, I participated in the European Sepsis Academy (ESA), a European Union funded project. Through this project I collaborated in medical research at the Amsterdam University Medical Center and University of Athens. I received my undergraduate degree in applied mathematics and physics from the New Jersey Institute of Technology.
